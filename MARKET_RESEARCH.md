## 2. Selected Niche: **Solo Residential Roofing Contractors**
*(with HVAC as a tested fallback)*

### Why roofing wins over HVAC
- **Higher Average Order Value:** $8k–$25k full roof replacement vs. $200–$8k HVAC repair. The unit economics on a single closed job pay for 6+ months of agency retainer — making your pitch math undeniable.
- **Cleaner urgency hook for Meta ads:** "Storm damage? Free inspection." A roof problem is binary (you have a leak or you don't). HVAC has more "is it broken or just old" ambiguity.
- **Insurance-claim layer:** Storm-restoration roofing leads can be 5–10x more valuable than retail. Lets you charge premium fast.
- **Lower technical sophistication:** Most solo roofers have a Wix site from 2017 and rely on door-knocking. Your bar to "look like a god" is very low.

### Why this niche beats the other three
1. **Math works on a single sale.** Cleaning client = ~$2k/yr LTV per household. Gym member = $1.5k/yr. Roofing job = $15k+ in one transaction. You can pay for a year of your retainer with a single closed lead.
2. **They already buy leads.** Angi/HomeAdvisor/CraftJack are $80–$200/lead and shared with 3–5 competitors. You're not creating a new behavior — you're replacing a hated one.
3. **Cultural fit for the pitch.** Roofers don't want fluff. They want phone calls. Your blueprint's "Revenue Recovery Engine" framing translates perfectly: leads → text → owner alert → closed deal. No abstraction.
4. **Seasonal urgency = compressed sales cycle.** Storm hits → owner panics → buys today. Compare to gym owner ("let me think about it until January") or realtor ("I'll see how my open house goes").
5. **Geographic moat.** Roofers serve a 30–50 mi radius. You can own a metro and stack case studies that don't compete with each other.

## 3. Micro-Niche Options Inside the Winner

Rank-ordered for Phase 1 attack:

| Micro-Niche | Why it's interesting | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| **#1 — Solo roofers in hail/storm metros** (TX, OK, CO, MO, NE, GA, NC, FL) | Insurance-claim work, ad creative writes itself ("After the [city] storm?"), seasonal panic. | Storm-chaser saturation in some markets — pick secondary cities, not Dallas/Denver core. |
| #2 — Solo roofers in older-housing metros (Midwest/Northeast: Cleveland, Pittsburgh, Buffalo, KC) | 50+ year roofs everywhere, less storm-chaser competition, "your roof is older than your kid" angle. | Less seasonal urgency, longer sales cycle. |
| #3 — HVAC repair (not install) shops in hot-summer metros (Phoenix, Vegas, Houston, Tampa) | Emergency-driven, 110°F = panic-buy, recurring maintenance plan upsell. | Smaller ticket, more competition from big chains. |
| #4 — Solo gutter installers / siding contractors | Adjacent + less competition than roofing, often run by the same owner. | Smaller market — harder to scale to 20 clients. |

**Recommended attack path:** Start with #1. Pick **one secondary metro** (e.g., Tulsa, Oklahoma City, Wichita, Springfield MO, Greensboro NC) — not a Tier-1 city. Stack 3 case studies there. Then expand laterally to similar metros.

## 4. Customer Pain — Real Language

### Core Problems
- Inconsistent month-to-month revenue (feast/famine)
- Paying $100+/lead to Angi/HomeAdvisor for leads shared with 4 competitors
- Door-knocking is exhausting and not scalable past the owner
- "Storm chasers" from out of state poach jobs in their market
- They're a roofer, not a marketer — their website is broken or invisible on Google
- Insurance adjusters increasingly denying claims, making sales harder

### Raw Verbatim Pain Language *(use this in ads, DMs, and the offer)*
Lift these as headlines, hooks, and email subject lines. Do **not** rewrite into "professional" copy.

- *"I'm a roofer, not a marketer."*
- *"My phone didn't ring once last week."*
- *"I closed 1 out of 30 Angi leads last month. It's a scam."*
- *"I'm sharing every lead with four other guys and racing to call first."*
- *"I had one great month and then July was crickets."*
- *"Every storm chaser from Texas shows up in my market and steals my jobs."*
- *"I need two full retails this month or I'm laying Miguel off."*
- *"My website looks like dog s\*\*\*. I know it does. I don't have time."*
- *"Door-knocking is killing my back and my marriage."*
- *"I'm working 70 hours a week and making less than my installer."*
- *"Adjusters are denying everything now. The insurance game is broken."*
- *"I do great work but nobody knows I exist."*

### Pain Bullets (3 layers)

**Surface Pain** — what they'll say out loud
- Not enough leads
- Leads from Angi are trash
- Website doesn't bring in calls
- Need more insurance-claim work

**Deeper Pain** — what they feel but won't say at a chamber-of-commerce mixer
- Embarrassed their marketing is so amateur compared to the big regional outfits
- Resentful of paying lead-platforms that treat them like a commodity
- Anxious that one slow month means laying off a guy who has a family
- Frustrated that they're better at the trade than the well-funded competitors but losing the marketing war

**Hidden Pain** — the 2 AM thoughts
- "Was leaving my W-2 to start this company a mistake?"
- "If next month is slow I have to dip into the kids' college savings."
- "My wife is starting to ask if we should sell the truck."
- "I'm 45 and my back can't keep me on a roof past 55."
- "If I don't figure out marketing soon, this business dies with me."

### Emotional Triggers
- **Fear:** Slow month → can't make payroll → personal humiliation
- **Frustration:** Working harder than the competition but losing
- **Pride:** "I do better work than these guys" — wants validation via volume
- **Envy:** Sees the big regional players running ads everywhere
- **Hope:** Wants to believe one good system fixes everything

### Midnight Thoughts *(use as ad hooks)*
- "If August is like July, I'm done."
- "Every other roofer in town has a sign on every lawn — what am I doing wrong?"
- "I should've stayed at [former employer]. At least I had benefits."
- "Why am I paying Angi $1,500 a month for nothing?"

## 5. Demand Validation

### Demand Signals (the proof people already pay for this outcome)
- **Angi / HomeAdvisor / CraftJack / Networx** — roofers spend $500–$5k/mo here despite hating it. Demand is **proven** at platform level.
- **Hatch, Roofr, AccuLynx, JobNimbus** — software companies built specifically for roofing sales. The category is mature enough to support multi-million dollar SaaS.
- **Facebook groups** — "Roofing Contractor Marketing," "Roofing Business Owners," 10k+ members each, daily lead-gen complaints and pitches.
- **Existing competitors** — Hook Agency, Roofing Sites, Lead PPC, Adkins Digital all charge $1,500–$5,000/mo for roughly this service. They exist and are profitable. **You're entering a validated category, not creating one.**
- **YouTube** — "Roofing leads" gets ~10k–50k searches/mo. Adam Bensman ("The Roof Strategist"), The Roofing CEO, etc. have large audiences = active buyer pool.

### Buyer vs. Browser

**Buyers** *(target these)*
- Solo / 2–10 employee roofing outfits, $300k–$1.5M annual revenue
- Currently paying for Angi/HomeAdvisor or running Google LSAs
- Active in private FB groups, attending IRE (International Roofing Expo) or local roofer meetups
- Have a CRM (even if just JobNimbus or a spreadsheet)

**Browsers** *(skip — will waste your pilot slots)*
- Side-hustle roofers with W-2 day jobs
- Pre-revenue / "thinking about starting"
- 1099 sales reps for big outfits (not decision-makers)
- Owners over ~$3M revenue who already have an in-house marketing person

### Demand Score
- **Urgency:** 9/10 — phone-doesn't-ring panic is immediate
- **Willingness to pay:** 9/10 — already paying $500–$5k/mo for inferior alternatives
- **Competition:** 7/10 — many agencies exist but most are generic "digital marketing" shops, not productized lead-gen with SMS speed-to-lead

## 6. Week 1 Validation Test Plan

Goal of Week 1: **prove enough demand to lock in 3 pilot clients within 14 days.** Sequence the three tests below in parallel, not serially.

### Test 1 — Pre-Sell DM/Cold Email (Primary)
**What:** Send 100 personalized outbound messages to solo roofers in 2–3 target secondary metros (e.g., Tulsa, OKC, Wichita).

**How to find them:**
- Google Maps scrape "roofer in [city]" → filter to 1–10 employee shops (avoid the franchises)
- State licensing board rosters (most states publish them)
- Facebook business pages with <2k followers in target metros

**Message script (text/IG DM, not email — higher open rate):**
> *"Hey [Name] — saw your work on [specific job from their FB/IG]. I build text-back lead systems for solo roofers and I'm picking 3 shops in [Metro] for a free 30-day pilot — I cover everything except $500 of Meta ad spend. If it doesn't bring you booked inspections, you don't pay me a dime, ever. I'm trading the work for a video testimonial if it hits. Worth a 10-min call this week?"*

**Success criteria:**
- ≥ 10% reply rate (10+ replies)
- ≥ 5 "tell me more" conversations
- ≥ 1 signed pilot by Day 7
- ≥ 3 signed pilots by Day 14

### Test 2 — Fake-Door Landing Page + Paid Traffic
**What:** Spin up a 1-page "Roofing Leads On Demand — [Metro] Pilot" landing page. CTA: "See if you qualify for the free 30-day pilot."

**Spend:** $100–$200 on FB/IG ads targeting "small business owners" + "roofing contractor" interests in target metros.

**Measure:**
- CTR ≥ 2% = hook is resonant
- Landing page opt-in ≥ 15% = offer language works
- Booked-call rate ≥ 25% of opt-ins = qualified intent

This test does double duty: it **validates the offer language** AND **becomes your pilot deliverable demo** ("here's the same page I'd build for you").

### Test 3 — 30 Loom Audits in 5 Days
**What:** Record 30 personalized 90-second Loom videos auditing roofers' current websites/Google Business profiles. "Here are 3 places you're leaking leads — happy to send you the fix even if we never work together."

**Why this works:**
- Builds your "Rule of 100" muscle from the Phase 2 blueprint early
- Loom DMs get 40%+ view rates vs. 5% for text
- The video itself is the proof you're competent
- Even non-converters often forward to friends → referral pipeline

**Success criteria:**
- ≥ 40% video view rate
- ≥ 15% reply rate
- ≥ 3 booked calls from the 30

### Combined Week 1 Pass/Fail

| Metric | Target | If hit | If missed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pilots signed | ≥ 1 in 14 days | Niche validated, proceed to offer build | Re-run with adjusted script before changing niche |
| Reply rate (outbound) | ≥ 10% | Pain language is right | Re-extract pain — your script doesn't match their voice |
| Booked calls (any source) | ≥ 5 in 14 days | Demand is real | Likely wrong micro-niche or wrong city |

**Fallback rule:** If after 200 outbound and 2 weeks you don't have a pilot signed in roofing — swap to HVAC repair (micro-niche #3) before changing the entire niche. The mechanics are nearly identical.
