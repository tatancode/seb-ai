# PRICING.md
*Pricing strategy for The Booked Roof Engine — Phase 1 of Ramsey AI*

---

## 1. Value Analysis — The Roofer's Math

### The outcome we deliver
- 10–20 **exclusive, qualified, booked roof-inspection appointments** on the owner's calendar per month
- Response time: under 45 seconds, 24/7/365
- Owner only has to show up to the roof

### What that outcome is worth (to the roofer)
This is the foundation every price has to anchor against.

| Input | Conservative | Realistic | Strong-month |
|---|---|---|---|
| Avg ticket (mix of repair + replacement) | $8,000 | $12,000 | $18,000 |
| Roofer's gross margin | 25% | 30% | 35% |
| Profit per closed job | **$2,000** | **$3,600** | **$6,300** |
| Inspection-to-close rate (qualified leads) | 15% | 25% | 35% |
| Booked inspections needed per closed job | ~7 | 4 | ~3 |

### Monthly value delivered (at our deliverable targets)

| Deliverable | Closed jobs (realistic @ 25%) | Revenue generated | **Profit generated** |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8 inspections/mo (Starter floor) | 2 | $24,000 | **$7,200** |
| 12 inspections/mo (Growth target) | 3 | $36,000 | **$10,800** |
| 18 inspections/mo (Pro target) | 4–5 | $54,000+ | **$16,200+** |

**The price-justification anchor:** We're going to charge between $1,500 and $4,000/month for an outcome that generates **$7k–$16k/month in pure profit**. That's a 4x–10x ROI on the retainer alone — the exact band Hormozi targets for grand-slam offers.

## 2. Delivery Model Impact

- **Model:** Done-for-you (DFY) — owner action is literally read texts + show up
- **Implication:** Premium pricing tier — DFY commands 2–4x the price of DIY/DWY services
- **Cost-to-deliver per client (after Phase 2 systemization with LATAM technical hire):** ~$300–$600/mo
- **Target gross margin:** 70–85% on the retainer line

## 3. Pricing Range

| | Floor | Recommended | Premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monthly retainer | $1,500 | $2,500 | $4,000 |
| Setup (new) | $0 (pilot grad) | $1,500 one-time | $1,500 one-time |

| Performance bonus per inspection above baseline | $250 | $300 | $400 |

**Anchoring against alternatives the roofer already pays:**
- Angi/HomeAdvisor: $1,200–$3,000/mo for *shared* low-intent leads → we're priced same-to-slightly-above for **exclusive, qualified, booked** appointments
- Generic local marketing agency: $1,500–$5,000/mo for vague "growth" → we deliver a *specific* unit (inspections), measurable weekly
- A regional roofer's in-house marketing person: $60k–$90k/yr salary + benefits → we replace at ~$30k/yr fully loaded
- One bad month of door-knocking labor (50 hrs/week × 4 weeks × $25/hr opportunity cost): ~$5,000/mo of the owner's own time

## 4. Recommended Pricing Strategy: **Margin (High-Ticket) with Hybrid Tier Structure**

**Reasoning:**
- Audience is small-business owners, not consumers — they expect retainers
- DFY service can't scale on volume — needs margin
- 3 clear tiers create decoy/contrast effect that funnels most prospects to middle tier
- Performance bonus add-on lets you punch through the retainer ceiling without scaring buyers with a higher base price

---

## 5. Pricing Tiers — **Locked Numbers**

### Tier 1 — **Starter** (the "yes I'll try this" tier)
- **For:** 1–3 person solo roofers, ~$300k–$600k revenue, currently spending $500–$1k/mo on Angi
- **Monthly:** **$1,500/mo**
- **Setup:** $1,500 one-time (waived for pilot graduates and 3-month prepay)
- **Ad spend:** $500–$1,000/mo client-funded, paid directly to Meta, at-cost
- **Baseline deliverable:** 6 booked inspections/mo
- **Performance bonus:** **$250 per booked-and-showed inspection above 6/month**, capped at $1,500/mo bonus
- **What's included:** Core ad library, branded landing page, 45-sec SMS responder, AI qualification, calendar booking, owner alerts, monthly strategy call, weekly report

### Tier 2 — **Growth** *(the workhorse, will be 70% of clients)*
- **For:** 4–10 person shops, ~$600k–$1.5M revenue, currently spending $1.5k–$3k/mo on lead platforms
- **Monthly:** **$2,500/mo**
- **Setup:** $1,500 one-time (waived for pilot graduates)
- **Ad spend:** $1,000–$2,500/mo client-funded, at-cost
- **Baseline deliverable:** 12 booked inspections/mo
- **Performance bonus:** **$300 per booked-and-showed inspection above 12/month**, capped at $2,500/mo bonus
- **What's included:** Everything in Starter, plus storm-trigger ad activation, dead-lead reactivation campaigns, bi-weekly strategy call, multi-creative ad rotation

### Tier 3 — **Pro** *(the ceiling for Phase 1; transitions into Phase 3 corporate tier)*
- **For:** Established shops, 8–15 employees, $1M–$3M revenue, multi-zip-code coverage
- **Monthly:** **$4,000/mo**
- **Setup:** $1,500 one-time
- **Ad spend:** $2,500–$5,000/mo client-funded, at-cost
- **Baseline deliverable:** 18 booked inspections/mo
- **Performance bonus:** **$400 per booked-and-showed inspection above 18/month**, capped at $4,000/mo bonus
- **What's included:** Everything in Growth, plus multi-metro coverage, dedicated account manager, weekly strategy call, custom retention campaigns, priority support

## 6. Setup Fee Strategy

**Decision: Charge $1,500 one-time setup, waive in 3 scenarios.**

| Scenario | Setup fee | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Pilot graduate converting to paid | **Waived** | Earned it — they took the leap with you first |
| 3-month prepay upfront | **Waived** | Trades upfront cash for the fee |
| 12-month prepay | **Waived** + 2 months free | Locks LTV |
| Direct-to-paid new client | **$1,500** | Filters tire-kickers, anchors value, funds your week-1 ops |

**Don't** waive setup as a sales tactic on every call — it cheapens it. Waiving it for pilot graduates is a *reward*, which is a stronger frame.

---

## 7. Ad Spend Pass-Through

**Decision: Client pays Meta directly. At cost. No markup.**

**Why:**
- Builds trust (no accusations of marking up ad spend)
- Removes a $1k–$5k/mo cashflow liability per client from your side
- Keeps your retainer numbers clean and defensible on a call
- Lets clients scale spend up/down without renegotiating with you
- Phase 3 you can re-evaluate — once you have enterprise clients, agency-managed ad accounts with 15% markup is standard

**Operationally:**
- You request access to their Meta Business Manager
- They keep the credit card on file
- Minimum $1,000/mo ad spend recommended (below this, math gets shaky)
- Pause/resume ad spend = client's call, but log it in the engagement agreement

## 8. Performance Bonus Structure

This is the unlock that makes the offer **irresistible** for performance-minded roofers and lets you punch through the retainer ceiling.

### How it works
1. Each tier has a **baseline** of booked-and-showed inspections per month (6 / 12 / 18)
2. For every inspection **above** baseline, you earn the per-inspection bonus ($250 / $300 / $400)
3. **"Booked-and-showed"** = lead booked an inspection AND the homeowner was actually there when the roofer arrived (filters no-shows)
4. Tracked in a shared dashboard, billed monthly with the retainer
5. Bonus cap = roughly the size of the retainer itself (so a great month roughly doubles their bill)

### Example — Growth tier client has a strong month
- Retainer: $2,500
- Delivered 18 booked-and-showed inspections (6 above baseline of 12)
- Performance bonus: 6 × $300 = **$1,800**
- Total bill to client: **$4,300/mo**
- Their value generated (at 25% close, $12k ticket): 18 × 25% × $12k = **$54,000 revenue**, ~$16,200 profit
- ROI for client: ~$16,200 profit / $4,300 cost = **3.8x ROI on a strong month** — still wildly defensible

### Why per-inspection beats per-closed-job
- You can't control their closing skill — paying you on closes means you eat their bad sales
- Inspection-booked is a unit YOU control end-to-end
- Tracking is clean — calendar event + show/no-show flag, no he-said/she-said

## 9. Annual vs Monthly Pricing

**Decision: Default to month-to-month. Offer prepay incentive but don't push hard in Phase 1.**

| Plan | Effective price | Pitch |
|---|---|---|
| Month-to-month | Full price | "No long-term commitment. Performance guarantee covers your risk." |
| 3-month prepay | Setup waived ($1,500 savings) | "Skip the setup, prove it works for a season" |
| 6-month prepay | 1 month free (~17% off) | "Lock in pilot pricing through storm season" |
| 12-month prepay | 2 months free (~17% off) | "Pay for 10, get 12" |

In Phase 1, don't lead with annual — the free pilot already proves your commitment, and you want clients to *want* to stay, not feel locked in.

## 10. The Pilot-to-Paid Transition

This is where 80% of agencies leak revenue. Don't.

### Timeline
- **Day 1:** Pilot kickoff. In the agreement, the post-pilot price is **explicitly written**: "Day 31 forward, retainer is $2,000/mo (locked pilot-graduate pricing for 6 months) or $2,500/mo standard."
- **Day 21:** Conversion conversation. Show them their results so far. Ask: "Do you want to keep this running?"
- **Day 28:** Confirm payment method. Setup ACH or card auto-charge.
- **Day 30:** Pilot ends. Day 31, retainer auto-charges.

### Pilot-graduate offer
> *"You took the leap with me first, so you get the founder rate forever — $2,000/mo for your first 6 months (vs. $2,500 standard), and your $1,500 setup fee is waived. The performance bonus applies from Day 31 forward."*

### What this does
- Frames the price *reduction* as a reward, not a discount you gave up
- Locks them in at a price that still has 70%+ margin for you
- Day 30 transition is a *renewal*, not a *sale* — much easier psychologically
- Removes the "what's it going to cost me?" anxiety because the number was on the agreement Day 1

## 11. Psychological Pricing

**Decision: Round numbers, not charm pricing.**

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| $1,500 / $2,500 / $4,000 — never $1,497 / $2,497 | Charm pricing signals consumer/low-trust. Round numbers signal B2B premium. |
| Always present highest tier first on written collateral | Anchors against Pro tier, makes Growth feel reasonable |
| On a sales call, lead with Growth as "what I recommend for you" | The decoy effect makes ~70% choose Growth |
| Stack value visibly: "$15k of components, $2,500/mo" | The gap between perceived value and price IS the offer |
| Present performance bonus as upside, never as a price increase | "You'll never pay more unless we deliver more" |

**Specific anchoring scripts to use on calls:**
- *"Your last Angi bill — what was it? [$1,800]. OK. For roughly that same number you get exclusive leads, replied-to in 45 seconds, on your calendar. Same dollar, completely different deliverable."*
- *"A regional roofer down in Dallas hires a marketing director for $85k a year. The Growth tier replaces 75% of what that hire would do, for about a third the cost."*
- *"One closed roof job covers four months of the retainer. The system has to deliver one job a quarter to pay for itself. Last month's pilots are seeing two to three a month."*

## 12. Price Justification Story (use on every sales call)

> *"Let's do the math. You said your average roof job is around $12k and you keep 30% of that — so call it $3,600 in profit per closed roof.*
>
> *On the Growth tier, you're paying $2,500 a month. So the system pays for itself the moment we book you one closed job per month.*
>
> *Our delivery target is 12 booked inspections a month. If you close 1 in 4 — which is below the industry average for a qualified, scheduled inspection — that's 3 closed jobs. Three jobs a month at $3,600 profit each is **$10,800/mo in profit on a $2,500/mo investment**.*
>
> *That's a 4x return on the retainer. And that's a normal month.*
>
> *Storm season, we hit 18, 20, 24 inspections. You can run the math from there.*
>
> *And if we don't hit at least 12 booked inspections in your first 60 days — we work for free until we do. So your downside is your $500 of ad spend a month and an hour of your time. Your upside is two to three full roofs a month, exclusive to you."*

## 13. Risk Check — Where the Pricing Could Break

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Client closes 0% of qualified inspections (their fault) | Performance guarantee is on **booked**, not closed — protects you. Sales playbook bonus helps protect *them*. |
| Ad spend underfunded → not enough volume | Hard floor: $1,000/mo ad spend minimum. Below that, decline the engagement. |
| Roofer is in a too-small market | Pre-qualify in sales call: metro population ≥ 100k. If smaller, decline or charge Starter only. |
| Price feels high to a $300k-revenue roofer | That's why Starter exists at $1,500 — it's the on-ramp. Don't discount Growth. |
| Performance bonus runs up and client gets sticker shock | Cap the bonus at the retainer size. They never pay more than 2x in any single month. |
| Pilot drifts into "free forever" | Hard Day-30 cutoff written into the pilot agreement. Day-21 conversion call mandatory. |
| Churn after Month 1 paid (client says "I can do this myself now") | Geographic exclusivity bonus + storm-trigger automation = real switching cost. Plus they don't actually want to run ads themselves — that's why they hired you. |

## 14. Pricing Experiments to Run

Once you have 5+ paid clients, A/B test these:

1. **Starter price test:** Does $2,000 close as well as $1,500? (likely yes — most roofers don't price-shop in $500 increments at this level)
2. **Setup fee test:** $1,500 vs. $2,500 vs. $999 — does conversion change?
3. **Performance bonus rate test:** $300/inspection vs. $400/inspection at Growth tier — does it change client satisfaction?
4. **Prepay incentive test:** "2 months free on annual" vs. "20% off annual" — same effective discount, different framing
5. **Tier-naming test:** Starter / Growth / Pro vs. Single Truck / Crew / Operation (more roofer-vernacular)

## 15. Path to $100k/Month — The Real Math

Three viable client mixes that hit $100k MRR. Run these scenarios when planning hiring and capacity.

### Scenario A — Volume play (35 clients)
| Tier | Count | MRR contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Starter @ $1,500 | 10 | $15,000 |
| Growth @ $2,500 | 20 | $50,000 |
| Pro @ $4,000 | 5 | $20,000 |
| Performance bonuses (avg $500/client) | 35 | $15,000 |
| **Total** | **35** | **$100,000** |

Pros: more case studies, more referrals.
Cons: 35 client conversations a month is a lot of ops.

### Scenario B — Balanced (25 clients) *(recommended)*
| Tier | Count | MRR contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Growth @ $2,500 | 18 | $45,000 |
| Pro @ $4,000 | 7 | $28,000 |
| Performance bonuses (avg $800/client) | 25 | $20,000 |
| Annual prepay buffer | — | $7,000 |
| **Total** | **25** | **$100,000** |

Pros: Manageable client count with a Phase-2 ops manager. High per-client revenue.
Cons: Each client churn hurts more.

### Scenario C — Enterprise (Phase 3 territory) (15 clients)
| Tier | Count | MRR contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Pro @ $4,000 | 10 | $40,000 |
| Corporate (Phase 3 tier @ $7,500) | 8 | $60,000 |
| **Total** | **18** | **$100,000** |

Pros: Lean ops, premium positioning.
Cons: Requires Phase 3 corporate avatar — not the goal for Phase 1.

**Recommended path:** Scenario B. Get there by Q4 of Year 1.

### Milestone trajectory

| Month | Clients | Tier mix | MRR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1–3 | 3 pilots | All pilots (free) | $0 |
| Month 4 | 3 → 5 | 3 graduates @ $2k + 2 new | ~$11k |
| Month 6 | 8 | Mostly Growth | ~$20k |
| Month 9 | 14 | Growth + first Pro | ~$38k |
| Month 12 | 20 | Scenario B mix | ~$60k |
| Month 18 | 25 | Scenario B at full bonus | **$100k** |

This is consistent with the blueprint's Phase 2 ($10–20k/mo) and Phase 3 ($100k/mo) milestones.
